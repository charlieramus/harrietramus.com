# ✍️ Write up a trip

Hi! This is the form for turning one of your trips into a page on the site.
There's **no app to learn and nothing technical** — you just write in this
document like a normal Google Doc, and Charlie turns it into the website page.

## How it works (read this once)

1. **Fill in the four lines at the top** (Title, Place, Trip, When).
2. **Write your story** in the "Story" area, in normal paragraphs.
3. **Wherever you want a photo to appear, start a new line with `PHOTO:`** and
   then a few words about it. That's the caption — and it's how Charlie knows
   which photo from your Drive folder goes there. Example:
   `PHOTO: the old man selling mangoes outside the market`
4. Put the actual photo files in the shared **Drive folder** (don't worry about
   order or names — the caption is enough for Charlie to match them).
5. If there's a sentence you *love* and want shown big, put it on its own line
   starting with `QUOTE:`. This is optional.

That's the whole thing. Write as much or as little as you like, and use as many
`PHOTO:` lines as you want, in any order.

When you're done, just tell Charlie — no need to send anything.

---

## 👀 Here's a finished example (so you can see it)

Title: Three Mornings in Rwanda
Place: Rwanda
Trip: Africa
When: February 2026 · 3 days

Intro:
We came for the gorillas and stayed for the mornings. Rwanda wakes up slowly and
green, and for three days we were up before it was, waiting in the mist for the
forest to decide whether it would show us anything.

Story:
The trek started in a village at the edge of the park, where kids walked us to
the treeline just to see the strangers off. The guides talked in low voices the
whole way up.

PHOTO: our guide pointing up the ridge into the fog

An hour in, the trees opened and there they were — a whole family, unbothered, a
baby rolling down a slope like it was the best thing anyone had ever invented.

PHOTO: a young gorilla mid-tumble down the hill

QUOTE: You don't get used to being looked at by something that knows exactly what you are.

We came down soaked and grinning and didn't say much. Some mornings you just
carry home in your chest.

PHOTO: the valley on the walk back, still full of cloud
